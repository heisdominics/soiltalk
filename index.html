The problem

Farmers are flying blind on their most important input.

Soil tests cost too much

Lab tests cost ₦15,000–₦30,000 and take weeks. Most smallholders never get one in their lifetime.

Wrong inputs, wasted money

Without soil data, farmers over-apply or under-apply fertilizer — losing up to 40% of yield potential.

Advice isn't local

Existing agri-apps give generic English tips. Most farmers need advice in Yoruba, Hausa, or Igbo to act on it.

How it works

Three steps from ground to guidance.

1

Push the probe into the soil

The $5 acoustic SoilTalk probe emits sound pulses into the earth. No batteries — it draws power from your phone's headphone jack or USB-C.

2

Open the app and press scan

The app captures acoustic return signals in under 30 seconds. Our AI model — fully on-device — interprets compaction, moisture levels, and nutrient density.

3

Get advice in your language

Plain-language recommendations delivered in Yoruba, Hausa, Igbo, or Pidgin. What to plant, how much to fertilize, when to water — tailored to your crop and region.
